## 8 expert-angle one-liners (use as pitch-back lines when journalist queries arrive)

These are framings, not full answers. When a Featured.com query arrives, Ali picks the closest angle + writes 80-120 words of expert response.

### 1. Long-stay tax angle
> "Serviced apartments crossed into a different tax bracket from hotels above 28 nights, after a 2018 HMRC clarification — corporate clients can sometimes deduct accommodation costs as a business expense in ways they can't with hotel bookings. The cost difference per night between a 4-star hotel and a serviced apartment narrows sharply past three weeks, and the financial calculus is one of the biggest reasons relocators switch."

### 2. Relocator vs hotel maths
> "For someone moving to London for a 6-week project, a serviced apartment averages 35-45% cheaper than the equivalent 4-star hotel by week 3. The crossover isn't just rate — it's that hotels charge for room service breakfast, laundry, and gym day-passes that come bundled in a serviced apartment as a kitchen, a washing machine, and a residential building."

### 3. Central London neighbourhoods by use-case
> "Central London isn't one place — it's seven London-villages stitched together. Marylebone/Regent's Park works for early-morning West End meetings; Old Street/Shoreditch for tech; Borough/Pimlico for banking + Whitehall; Kensington for the museum cluster + South Ken corporates. Picking the right neighbourhood for a 4-week stay saves 30-45 minutes per day in Tube time."

### 4. The 90-day STR rule (Greater London)
> "London's Deregulation Act 2015 caps short lets in residential properties at 90 nights/year — anything above triggers a change-of-use planning permission. Legitimate serviced apartment operators run on commercial leases or have C1/Sui Generis planning consent, which is why their cost profile differs from typical Airbnb hosts. When a property says 'unlimited stays', either it's a hotel, or it has the right planning permission, or it's quietly non-compliant — and that matters for corporate procurement teams."

### 5. The cleaning-frequency reality
> "Hotel-style daily housekeeping in serviced apartments is mostly a myth in 2026 — most operators (us included) clean fortnightly on long stays, with mid-stay cleans available on request. The reason is double: guests on 3-week stays usually don't WANT daily housekeeping (it's intrusive), and the labour economics don't support it at a competitive rate. Daily housekeeping is a paid add-on, like a hotel club lounge."

### 6. The all-bills-included pricing structure
> "Most London hotels look cheaper on a comparison site but add 15-25% in unbundled extras — Wi-Fi if not Hilton Honors, breakfast at £18-25, laundry per item, gym fee. Serviced apartments quote all-in: heating, council tax, Wi-Fi, electricity, water, often a welcome basket. For a 28-night stay, that 'looks-cheaper' hotel quote is usually £400-700 short of the realised cost."

### 7. Self check-in operations (the real picture)
> "Smart locks are now standard in serviced apartments — code by WhatsApp, no front desk. Where it gets nuanced: about 30% of older London buildings (especially Listed buildings or short-leasehold blocks) don't allow smart-lock installation, so those properties stay on in-person key handover at a time the guest chooses. The honest answer to 'do you have smart locks' is 'on most apartments, yes — some are key handover, we'll tell you which before you book'."

### 8. Why direct beats OTA on long stays
> "Booking.com and Airbnb charge operators 15-18% commission, which gets passed to the guest in higher headline rates. On a 1-week stay the difference is £20-40; on a 6-week stay it's £400-700. Direct booking through the operator's site usually saves 10-15% AND gives the guest a single point of contact for the entire stay — which matters when something needs fixing on night 18."
